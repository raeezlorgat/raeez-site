Raeez Lorgat

"The universe is written in the language of mathematics." — Galileo

In 2009 I dropped out of MIT to help build the payments infrastructure that became Stripe. We cold-called banks and wrote the code that would eventually process hundreds of billions of dollars.

After Stripe, I returned to the mathematics and physics that had occupied me since childhood—spending a decade at MIT and the Perimeter Institute for Theoretical Physics working on arithmetic geometry, factorization algebras, and the geometry of quantum field theory. The mathematics I study—how local structures compose into global ones—shapes how I think about institutional design.

Now I'm building Mass—programmable jurisdiction infrastructure that turns incorporation, compliance, and banking into APIs. Mass is the operating system for a new kind of decentralized network, liveu across the US, Cayman, BVI and the Special Economic Zone Próspera ZEDE on the island of Roatán, Honduras. In 2026 and beyond we're expanding into Abu Dhabi, Dubai, Kazakhstan, Korea, Indonesia and China.
